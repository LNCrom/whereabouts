# Whereabouts Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 18, 2026

Whereabouts is a personal family location dashboard built around consent and member-controlled sharing.

## Data Collection and Use

Whereabouts uses consent-based location sharing. When you create or accept a Whereabouts invite and enable location sharing, the app may store the following information in Apple's iCloud/CloudKit service so invited family members can view it in the app:

- Your device display name
- Your current latitude and longitude
- The time your location was last updated
- A random device identifier generated by Whereabouts
- A random invite/circle code used to connect approved family members

Whereabouts does not sell personal data and does not use location data for advertising.

Contact selection, when used, helps you choose people to invite. Whereabouts does not upload your address book to the developer.

## Location Data

Whereabouts is designed for consent-based family location sharing. The app does not provide hidden tracking and does not access private Apple Find My location data. Apple does not make Find My people-location data available to third-party apps.

Sharing requires the person sharing their location to install/open Whereabouts, accept an invite, and grant iOS location permission. You can pause sharing in the app or revoke location permission in iOS Settings.

## Children and Families

Whereabouts is intended for personal family coordination. Parents or guardians should manage consent for children in their family group.
